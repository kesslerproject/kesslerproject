The Life of Virginia D'Albert-Lake

1940

June 23

  • Capitulation of France. D'Albert-Lake was an American expat living in France at the time, so this capitulation affected her and her husband (a French soldier) greatly.

September

  • Virginia and her husband, Philippe, move to Nesles after the dismantling of the French government.

1943

Fall

  • Virginia and Philippe are introduced to the Comet Escape Line by their baker, Mr. Renard. They help their contacts transport stranded American airmen out of Nazi France back to England.

1944

Before June

  • Transporting men back to England becomes to dangerous. Because of this, the Comet Escape Line decides to instead keep the men in camps that are hidden around France, with the hope that they will be liberated by the advancing Allied armies.

June 15

  • Virginia is arrested becuase of her involvemement with the French Resistance and her American citizenship. She is interrogated by German officials on her involvement and the specifics of the route.

June 15 - August 15

  • Viriginia is imprisoned at Fresnes and Romainville. While in the prison, she was stripped of all personal belongings and was made to suffer generally poor conditions.

August 15 - August 22

  • Virginia is deported to Ravensbrück. She is transported along with the ohter prisoners in an overcrowded bus, and then in a train car that was completely filled.
  • When she was made to change trains, the Red Cross was there wil boiled potatoes and milk to aid the prisoners.

August 22 - October 16

  • Virginia arrives at Ravensbrück, and is imprisoned there until she is moved. At Ravensbrück, she had to endure conditions that were commonat the camps such as starvation, the cold, improper clothing, and being surrounded by misery and death.

October 16

  • Virginia is moved from Ravensbrück to Königsberg. She wishes to never see Ravensbrück again. At Königsberg, they were made to work in freezing conditions. The cold and the starvation eventually causes people's bodies to stop functioning properly.

1945

February 2

  • Virginia returns to Ravensbrück along with the other prisoners. At Ravensbrück, she feels herself getting weaker and weaker due to the conditions of the camps.

February 28

  • Virginia is transported to Liebenau Internment Camp. She is held here until she is liberated by the Allied Army.

April 21

  • Allied Army enteres Liebenau, and liberates it.
